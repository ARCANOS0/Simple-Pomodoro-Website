What's Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique: A Powerful Time Management Method The Pomodoro Technique is a simple yet effective time management method designed to improve focus, efficiency, and productivity. Developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s, this technique is based on breaking work into timed intervals, known as Pomodoros (Italian for "tomatoes"), with short breaks in between.

How the Pomodoro Technique Works ?

Choose a Task: Select a task or project that you want to work on. Set a Timer: Traditionally, the timer is set for 25 minutesâ€”this is one Pomodoro. Work on the Task: Focus completely on your work without any distractions until the timer rings. Take a Short Break: After completing one Pomodoro (25 minutes of focused work), take a 5-minute break to recharge. Repeat the Process: Complete four Pomodoros, then take a longer break (15-30 minutes). Continue the Cycle: Repeat the process as needed until you complete your work.

Customizing the Pomodoro Technique

While the standard Pomodoro session lasts 25 minutes, you can adjust the duration based on your preference. Some people prefer 50-minute work sessions with 10-minute breaks or other variations depending on the task and personal productivity style.
